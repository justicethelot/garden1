About

Impression is a time sensitive, web-based work about the natural transition of space, both online and offline. Impressions are both physical, marks or indentations made in a space and abstract, sensations or perceptions. In web analytics terms, an impression refers to the number of times a piece of content is seen (views).

Impression changes over the course of its life, starting off as something resembling an overgrown, empty lot or abandoned webpage, full of broken images, trash, and weeds (invasive plants). Visitors are invited to take one of four actions: pull a weed, plant a flower, pick up trash or litter. As time goes by and visitor counts rise, the page will change, both from direct actions taken by visitors and indirect changes programmed into the website’s code. As the webpage or empty lot, gets nurtured by clicks and views, it becomes functional, broken images slowly reappear to show a garden.

All of this expansion and forward movement takes the webpage into its next natural progression, for profit. The garden becomes a storefront. The invasive plants that once littered the empty webpage are now being sold for exorbitant prices. What occurs naturally eventually becomes commodified. The cycle continues indefinitely, as the website resets and moves in and out of its various stages of decline and optimization.
